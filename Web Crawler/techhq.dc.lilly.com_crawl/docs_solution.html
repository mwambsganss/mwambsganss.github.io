Tech Positioning‚Äã


Tech positioning is the strategic process of aligning technology (including IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, tools, accelerators,
components, and features) with Lilly‚Äôs goals and business needs. This ensures that every technology investment supports

Technology Classifications
We use the following technology classifications to describe the maturity, adoption, and strategic relevance of
technologies in Tech@Lilly:
    

  • Emerging ‚Äì Technologies or capabilities in the early stages of exploration and experimentation. These are
typically undergoing proof-of-value, pilot programs, or viability testing to assess potential business impact
and feasibility.
    • 

  • Specialized ‚Äì Solutions that are tailored to specific use cases, departments, or business units. They are not
broadly adopted across the enterprise but deliver high value in their niche applications.
    • 

  • Standard ‚Äì Technologies or capabilities that are approved for enterprise-wide use. They are well-supported,
stable, and scalable, with established governance, documentation, and integration patterns.
    • 

  • Strategic ‚Äì Foundational technologies or platforms that are critical to the enterprise‚Äôs long-term strategy
and operations. These receive ongoing investment, are deeply embedded in business processes, and are central to
innovation, scalability, and resilience.
    • 

  • Declining ‚Äì Technologies or capabilities that are no longer aligned with strategic direction. Investment is
being reduced, and a retirement or replacement plan is being developed or considered.
    • 

  • Retiring ‚Äì Solutions that are in the active process of being phased out. Replacement technologies have been
identified, and migration or decommissioning efforts are underway.
    • 

  • Exited ‚Äì Technologies or capabilities that have been fully retired. They are no longer supported, approved, or
in use within the enterprise environment.
    • 

  • Prohibited ‚Äì Technologies or practices that are explicitly blocked or restricted due to substantial risk,
compliance issues, or a formal enterprise architecture decision. Use is not permitted under any circumstances.
    • 



Tech positioning plays a key role in optimizing our technology landscape, mitigate risks, and minimizing the cost of
change. It is designed for architects and technical leaders responsible for delivering products and solutions and for
aligning technology with strategic business goals.
